Apple’s “Scary Fast” Showcase: Major Announcements

The sense of introspection regarding Fitbit's integration into Google over the past year. The Fitbit Charge 6, introduced as a potential return to form, is examined with a critical lens.

The reintroduction of the side button, absent in the Charge 5, becomes a focal point. Disappointingly, the button turns out to be a haptic one, lacking the satisfying click of a mechanical button. Despite functionality, the author laments the absence of the beloved physical button experience, citing potential usability concerns for those with limited dexterity.

The Charge 6's fitness and health tracking capabilities receive praise, aligning with Fitbit's reputation in this domain. However, GPS tracking is deemed inferior to competitors like Garmin, occasionally overreporting runs. The addition of gym equipment integration is acknowledged as a positive, despite limited compatibility.

The software, now bearing Google's influence, triggers mixed reactions. Legacy Fitbit users are required to migrate to a Google Account, a process causing discontent. The app redesign, reminiscent of Material You, faces criticism from longtime users for eliminating features and reducing readability. The author notes Google's responsiveness to user feedback but highlights instances that could have been avoided.

The Charge 6's app selection, featuring Google Maps, YouTube Music, and Google Wallet, is scrutinized. The imposition of a $11 monthly subscription for YouTube Music and limited app choices raise concerns. The utilitarian nature of Google Wallet and Google Maps on the small screen is acknowledged.

Despite reservations, the author appreciates the Charge 6's existence in a market increasingly favoring smartwatches. However, the $160 price tag is deemed high, especially considering subscription costs. The success of the Charge 6 is seen as contingent on users embracing Google's vision for wearables, acknowledging the ongoing Google-fication of Fitbit. The author reflects on the shifting landscape while expressing a nostalgic sentiment for the Fitbit of old.
