This Pill Tracks Your Vitals From the Inside

Celero Systems is developing an electronic pill, resembling a biocompatible plastic capsule, that measures vital signs such as heart rate, breathing rate, and core temperature from within the human stomach. Initially designed for home monitoring of ongoing conditions, the pill could potentially serve as an internal alarm system for drug-related overdoses in the future.

In a small clinical trial, the pill was tested on sleep apnea patients, offering an alternative to polysomnography, the current diagnostic standard. The pill, wirelessly transmitting measurements to a laptop, proved nearly as accurate as traditional methods, with minimal discomfort and no reported side effects.

Celero's ingestible device, unlike wearables, directly measures lung movements internally. CEO Ben Pless, with a background in medical implants, sees the ingestible approach as unobtrusive and convenient, providing access to the body without surgery.

The pill's ability to record vitals inside the undulating gastrointestinal tract is noteworthy. The Celero team's signal processing techniques successfully separated heartbeats and breaths from digestive system noise, showcasing the pill's effectiveness in monitoring cardiac and respiratory activity in humans.

Beyond sleep apnea, the pill holds potential applications for unobtrusive at-home monitoring of heart- or breathing-related events in conditions like asthma, cardiac issues, and neuromuscular disorders. The remote tracking capability could enable doctors to mail pills to patients for continuous monitoring.

Celero Systems envisions the pill's transformative impact in detecting drug overdoses, a critical concern amid rising opioid-related deaths. The pill's regular measurements trigger alerts when breaths fall below a threshold, potentially facilitating timely intervention and reducing fatalities.

Preliminary studies on safety and efficacy in human volunteers showed promising results, with participants in a substance use disorder treatment program successfully swallowing the pill without side effects.

While digital tracking pills aren't entirely new, Celero's approach aims to address specific needs, including potentially delivering an overdose-reversing drug. The pill's unobtrusive nature, especially for individuals battling addiction, is seen as an advantage over wearables that can be easily forgotten or misplaced.

Celero Systems plans to refine tests on sleep apnea, expanding participant numbers and exploring longer-term body retention. With additional studies underway, the company aims to bring the transformative monitoring pill to market within the next few years.
