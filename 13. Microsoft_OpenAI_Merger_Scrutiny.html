Microsoft and OpenAI merging faces scrutiny

The aftermath of management changes at OpenAI has led to an increased role for Microsoft and a seat on the board, prompting the UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) to launch an inquiry. The CMA is investigating whether the closer relationship between OpenAI and Microsoft qualifies as a "relevant merger situation," even though no outright merger or acquisition has taken place.

The CMA's "Invitation to Comment" marks the initial step in the information-gathering process. Both companies and third parties can provide feedback for the CMA to consider before deciding on further actions, such as a phase 1 investigation, which could potentially result in decoupling the entities.

The term "relevant merger situation" encompasses various relationships impacting market competition, including minority shareholding and commercial arrangements. Microsoft's substantial investment in OpenAI, giving it just under 50% ownership, and their collaborative work on AI services, including Microsoft's Azure cloud platform, fall within this scope.

The CMA expresses concern about the partnership's impact on competition in the AI space, where OpenAI and Microsoft are influential players. Recent governance changes at OpenAI, involving Microsoft's participation, have drawn attention and triggered the CMA's scrutiny.

The inquiry will assess criteria under the country's Enterprise Act, considering factors such as the distinctiveness of the businesses, revenue generated through their relationship, and whether they collectively account for more than 25% of the market. The investigation could shed light on the partnership and the activities of both companies, providing a basis for future regulatory considerations.

Legal experts suggest that even if the CMA does not proceed with a full investigation, the preliminary inquiry enables a better understanding of governance arrangements within the OpenAI project, informing broader oversight of the rapidly developing AI sector.
