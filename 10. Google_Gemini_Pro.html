The article reflects on the evolving concept of the metaverse and its promises, discussing the technological challenges and the adaptability of the vision. It notes the initial enthusiasm and subsequent disenchantment with the metaverse, emphasizing the continual advancements in technology. The author draws a parallel with religious eschatologies, highlighting the believers' adaptation and reinterpretation of prophecies that do not materialize.

The comparison extends to the theological concept of "inaugurated eschatology," where believers experience glimpses of paradise in the present while anticipating its full realization in the future. The author relates this framework to Mark Zuckerberg's vision of the metaverse, perceiving a similar blend of existing mixed reality technologies and the promise of a fully immersive virtual paradise.

The article contemplates the human tendency to view technological aspirations as eschatological narratives, emphasizing the belief in progress leading to a transformative future. It explores the allure of the metaverse in delivering a seamless relationship with reality and suggests reconsidering the focus on the future for salvation. Drawing inspiration from Christ's statement that "the kingdom of God is within you," the author encourages recognizing the inner world of the human imagination as the original metaverse, readily accessible without external devices.

The author concludes by highlighting the potential richness of the inner world, shaping religious longings, artistic wonders, and technological inventions. The metaverse, originating as a conceptual idea, is seen as an extension of the human imagination, accessible anytime without the need for expensive headsets.
