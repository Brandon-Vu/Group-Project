Urbanista's Malibu Solar Speaker: Never Run Out of Power

Swedish audio brand Urbanista, known for its innovative solar-powered audio products, has unveiled the Malibu, a portable wireless speaker featuring Exegerâ€™s Powerfoyle solar cells. Priced at $149, the Malibu can recharge indoors and outdoors using ambient light and has an IP67 waterproof rating.

The rugged design houses a 3,600-mA battery, powering dual 10-watt amplifiers. The speaker, available in black and Desert Gray, can be paired with another Malibu for stereo sound. The accompanying app allows users to customize the sound profile and monitor live light-charging and historical data.

Urbanista claims the solar speaker has a full-day battery reserve, equivalent to 30 hours of playtime. In testing, the Malibu extended its playtime to 45 hours with 50,000 Lux solar light charging, akin to an average sunny day. The Powerfoyle solar cells demonstrate superior efficiency, allowing the speaker to recharge during periods of non-use.

Seven hours of use per week in complete darkness lasts up to 30 days. Seven hours of use per week in well-lit conditions lasts up to six months before traditional recharge. Seven hours of use per week in direct sunlight, plus standby time, should "never run out of battery power."

Giovanni Fili, CEO of Exeger, notes that Powerfoyle solar cells have seen efficiency improvements, including a nearly 50 percent increase in indoor efficiency and a 20 percent boost in outdoor efficiency in direct light. Fili also mentions enhancements for durability and sustainability in future products.

In conclusion, Urbanista's Malibu solar speaker offers a promising solution for prolonged battery life, showcasing advancements in solar technology and efficiency.
